  <h2>TYPES OF BIRIYANI</h2>

  <p style="color:#EF9A9A">All biryani have rice in common, but that’s about it! There are many different kinds of

    biryani that feature different meats, spices, and cooking styles. Here are just a few variations of this dish..</p>

  <dl>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Kolkata biryani</u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF">Kolkata biryani is flavored with mild spices in the rice and spicy chicken and originates

      from, you guessed it, Kolkata, India</dd>

    <br>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Sindhi Biryani</u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF">Sindhi biryani originated from Sindh (modern-day Pakistan) and gets its bold flavor from

      chilies and spices and has potatoes added in as well.</dd>

    <br>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Malabar biryani</u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF">Malabar biryani comes from the Malabar region of Kerala and features a paste made of green

      chiles, cinnamon, cloves, fennel, cardamom, and peppercorns.Another popular Kerala dish, Thalassery biryani uses

      roasted cashews for a nutty flavor. Thalassery biryani also uses short grain rice instead of long grain.</dd>

    <br>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Ambur biryani</u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF ">Ambur biryani is also made with short grain rice and gets a lot of its flavor from the

      chicken added to the dish as the rice is mildly spiced.</dd>

    <br>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Dindigul biryani </u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF ">Dindigul biryani has a tangy flavor thanks to lemon and curd. This biryani usually has

      lamb or chicken incorporated in it.</dd>

    <br>

    <dt style="color:#DAF7A6"><u>Memoni biryani</u></dt>

    <dd style="color:#9FE2BF">Similar to Sindhi biryani, Memoni Biryani is made with potatoes, chilies, and spices, but

      typically also uses lamb and tomatoes. Hyderabadi biryani Hyderabadi biryani comes in two forms: Pakki (cooked)

      and Kacchi (raw). Here, you cook the rice and meat separate from each other and then combine. Whether you’re

      laying the raw or cooked meat in between the rice, both kinds are then slow-cooked to perfection.</dd>

  </dl>
